Understand the key metrics that drive business decisions. This guide explains each financial ratio used in the application.

Profitability ratios measure a company's ability to generate earnings relative to its revenue, operating costs, balance sheet assets, or shareholders' equity over time.

Net Profit Margin

What it measures: The percentage of revenue left after all expenses, including taxes and interest, have been deducted. It's a key indicator of a company's overall profitability.

Formula: (Net Profit / Total Revenue) * 100

Learn More
Return on Assets (ROA)

What it measures: How efficiently a company is using its assets to generate profit. A higher ROA indicates better asset management.

Formula: (Net Profit / Total Assets) * 100

Learn More

Liquidity ratios measure a company's ability to pay off its short-term debts and obligations without raising external capital.

Current Ratio

What it measures: A company's ability to cover its short-term liabilities with its short-term assets. A ratio under 1 suggests potential difficulty in meeting obligations.

Formula: Current Assets / Current Liabilities

Learn More

Solvency ratios measure a company's ability to meet its long-term financial obligations. They are used to gauge a company's financial health and risk of bankruptcy.

Debt-to-Equity Ratio

What it measures: The proportion of a company's financing that comes from debt versus equity. A high ratio indicates high leverage and potentially higher risk.

Formula: Total Debt / Total Equity

Learn More
Interest Coverage Ratio (ICR)

What it measures: How easily a company can pay interest on its outstanding debt. It is a measure of how many times a company can cover its current interest payments with its available earnings.

Formula: EBITDA / Interest Expense

Learn More

Cash flow ratios measure a company's ability to generate cash to cover its debts and fund its operations.

Operating Cash Flow Ratio

What it measures: How well a company can pay off its current liabilities with the cash generated from its core business operations. A ratio above 1 is generally considered good.

Formula: Operating Cash Flow / Current Liabilities
